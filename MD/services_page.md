— SECTION 6 — ROLE-BASED PATHWAYS  ← EXPANDED TO 5 LEVELS —
⚑ CHANGE: 5 levels now, not 4. Entry Level added as distinct from Staff. Rationale: someone just joining (intern, probation, first year) has different capability needs from an established staff member who is not yet a team lead. They need onboarding, orientation, and foundation-building — not the same accountability and delivery-focused content as experienced staff.

The Right Capability,
For the Right Level.
We do not deliver the same programme to everyone. Every engagement is mapped to the roles in the room. These are the five levels we build for — each with distinct capability needs and different performance constraints.

LEVEL 1   Entry Level
Interns, probationers, and first-year employees. Just entering the organisation. The focus is orientation, professional foundations, and building the habits and mindsets that will shape their performance for years to come.
Professional conduct and workplace expectations
Communication basics — written, verbal, and digital
Learning to learn: feedback, reflection, and self-management
Understanding how organisations work
AI and digital fundamentals for the modern workplace
Entry Level is distinct from Staff. Someone on probation or in their first year is still forming their professional identity and workplace habits. They need different support from someone who is established in role.

LEVEL 2   Staff
Established individual contributors who are not yet managing others. In role, performing, but with room to grow in effectiveness, accountability, and capability.
Professional effectiveness and communication
Personal accountability and productivity systems
Digital capability and AI workflow integration
Service mindset and stakeholder etiquette
Cross-team collaboration and self-leadership

LEVEL 3   Team Leads & Supervisors
The first level of people responsibility. The person others report to — the line manager or supervisor. Managing up, managing across, and managing a team simultaneously. Often the most under-supported level in any organisation.
Running effective teams and structured meetings
Execution rhythm, KPI ownership, and delivery discipline
Coaching basics, feedback, and performance conversations
Process thinking and continuous improvement
Managing upwards and across the organisation

LEVEL 4   Managers & Unit Heads
Accountable for a function, unit, or department. Responsible for translating strategy into execution and managing the performance of a team of teams. Where most delivery pressure concentrates.
Translating strategy into execution plans
Cross-functional influence and stakeholder management
Decision making, risk, and issue management
Building performance culture and accountability systems
Developing people beneath them — not just managing them

LEVEL 5   Senior Leaders & Executives
Setting direction, building culture, and making decisions at organisational scale. The focus shifts from doing and managing to enabling, governing, and sustaining high performance across the whole system.
Strategic leadership, governance, and operating model clarity
Building bench strength and succession depth
Executive cadence and performance systems
Digital and AI readiness — adoption strategy and capability scaling
Leading through change and organisational complexity
